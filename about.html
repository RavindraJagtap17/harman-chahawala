From Streets to Steaming Cups

The 32-year journey of how a determined boy from Sangli built one of India's most loved tea franchise brands.

The Founder

Haribhau Kulkarni — Born in Hardship, Raised by Resilience

Haribhau Kulkarni lost his father at just two years old, leaving his family to survive the unforgiving streets of Sangli, Maharashtra. Where life threw challenges, Hari brewed hope.

At the age of 22, he began selling tea — not just as a livelihood, but as a dream in the making. Decades of relentless dedication, sacrifice, and an unshakeable belief in quality turned that dream into a legacy.

In 2018, with his son Siddharth by his side, he founded Harman Chahawala — a brand steeped in struggle, strength, and the spirit of self-belief. From a humble roadside stall to a respected name in Indian tea, his 32-year journey is more than business — it's a story of grit, grace, and greatness in every cup.

"We don't just serve tea. We serve a promise — that every cup deserves your trust."
